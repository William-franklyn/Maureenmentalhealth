Immediate Mental Health Assistance

If you are in crisis or experiencing distressing thoughts, please reach out immediately. You can contact the following confidential resources 24/7. **If you are in immediate danger, please call your local emergency number (e.g., 911, 112, 999) or go to the nearest emergency room.**

Simple Coping Strategies

  • Deep Breathing: Inhale slowly through your nose, hold for a few seconds, and exhale slowly through your mouth. Repeat several times to calm your nervous system.
  • Mindful Observation: Focus on your senses. Name 5 things you see, 4 things you feel, 3 things you hear, 2 things you smell, and 1 thing you taste.
  • Physical Activity: Engage in light exercise like walking, stretching, or yoga. Movement can significantly boost your mood.
  • Connect with Someone: Talk to a trusted friend, family member, or peer supporter about how you're feeling.
  • Limit News & Social Media: If feeling overwhelmed, take breaks from constant information streams.
  • Practice Gratitude: Take a moment to think about or write down things you are thankful for, however small.
  • Engage in a Hobby: Spend time doing something you enjoy, like reading, listening to music, drawing, or gardening.
